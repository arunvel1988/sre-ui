üì° Availability

Availability measures the percentage of time a system is operational and accessible. Commonly expressed in "nines" (e.g., 99.9% uptime).

üéØ Service Level Objectives (SLOs)

An SLO is a target level of reliability for a service, agreed upon by stakeholders. Example: 99.9% uptime per month.

üí∞ Error Budget

The error budget is the amount of downtime or failures allowed before breaching the SLO. Example: For 99.9% uptime, the monthly error budget is ~43 minutes.

üî• Burn Rate

Burn rate measures how quickly the error budget is being consumed. A high burn rate means issues are happening too often or for too long.

üìä Key Practices

  • Set realistic SLOs based on business needs.
  • Monitor burn rate to anticipate SLO breaches.
  • Balance reliability and development velocity using error budgets.
  • Automate alerting and response to stay within SLO limits.
